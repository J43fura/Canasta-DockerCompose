Hedi Slimane (French pronunciation: ​[eˈdi sliˈman]; born July 5, 1968) is a French photographer and grand couturier. From 2000 to 20072008, he held the position of creative director for Dior Homme (the menswear line of Christian Dior). From 2012 to 2016, he was the creative director for Yves Saint Laurent.[1][2]↵Since February 1, 2018, Slimane has been the creative, artistic and main image director of Celine.[3]

In November 2018, Hedi Slimane topped the annual list of Vanity Fair's "50 most influential FrenchFrench people in the world".[4]

Early life

Slimane was born in the 19th arrondissement of Paris to a Tunisian fatherTunisian father, who was an accountant, and an Italian mother, a dressmaker.[5][6] Two of his uncles were tailors.[6] In his youth, he and his mother made clothes together: Slimane sketched designs, the two would shop for fabrics and complete fittings, and his mother would cut and sew them.[6] He considered finding a career in the Fashion industry as too competitive, instead, he studied Political Science and pursued a career in Journalism.[6][7] At 11, he discovered photography, received his first camera and learned black and white darkroom printing. He studied Art History at the École du Louvre and completed a tailor apprenticeship at a men's designer house.[8]
